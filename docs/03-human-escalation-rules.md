# Human Escalation Rules

When the AI hands off to a human, when it keeps going, and when it stops.

## Escalate to a human immediately

- Prospect says they want to talk to a person, or “can I speak with someone?”
- Prospect is upset or frustrated (tone/keywords we flag).
- Prospect mentions legal, compliance, or contract terms.
- Prospect says they’re from a key account we already have (or a target logo)—we don’t want the bot to mis-handle them.
- Prospect’s role or ask doesn’t fit our flow (e.g. press, partnership, existing customer with a support issue)—route to the right team.
- Any request we don’t have a clear script or rule for and that feels high-stakes.

When we escalate, we pass context: what we know (org, role, BANT answers so far, last message). Human takes it from there.

## Continue automation

- Prospect is answering BANT normally and hasn’t asked for a human.
- Prospect is in nurture (yellow); we’re sending content and light touch follow-up per cadence.
- Prospect gave a red; we’ve sent the “not the right fit right now” message. No further outreach for a meeting—automation “continues” only in the sense that we don’t escalate; we just stop.

## Stop engaging

- We’ve disqualified (red on Budget, Authority, or Need). Don’t keep asking for a meeting or over-nurture.
- Prospect asked to be removed from communications. Honor it and stop.
- Prospect has gone silent for X days (we define X in nurture rules, e.g. 3+ no-reply touches) and we’ve already tried a final “still interested?”—then stop that sequence. Don’t spam.
- Prospect clearly not a fit (wrong segment, wrong product, spam). One polite outbound, then stop.

We stop to protect both the prospect and our team’s time. The agent doesn’t argue or re-pitch after a red or a clear “no.”
